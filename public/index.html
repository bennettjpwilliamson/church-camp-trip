Where to Stay

Three options

Tent in the group site

Pitch your tent in our reserved area. Picnic table and fire ring at every spot. Covered by the $10 / $20 contribution.

RV or trailer in the group site

Park in our reserved area. No hookups — self-contained rigs only. Generators allowed 9 AM – 9 PM. Covered by the $10 / $20 contribution.

Cabin or A-frame at the Plunge

If you'd rather sleep indoors, Silver Creek Plunge has A-frames from $69, family cabins from $89, and deluxe units from $119 per night. Limited availability — book early at silvercreekplunge.com. Note these are paid directly to the Plunge and are not covered by the church.

The Area

Silver Creek & the Plunge

The campground sits in the Boise National Forest at about 4,490 feet, on Silver Creek. A short walk from camp brings you to Silver Creek Plunge — a family-run mountain camp with a geothermal pool, lodge, store, and rental cabins.

Nearby and on-site

  • Geothermal pool — spring-fed, around 100°F. Day passes are $10 adult / $8 kid, paid at the Plunge office.
  • Hiking — Peace Creek, Silver Creek Summit, and Bull Creek trails leave from near camp.
  • Fishing — Silver Creek is stocked with rainbow trout. Bring a pole and an Idaho license.
  • Store — snacks, ice, firewood, propane, swimsuit and towel rentals at the Plunge.
  • Stargazing — no light pollution. The Milky Way is clearly visible.

Property map

Our group site sits east of the bridge, just north of the volleyball field. The pool, store, and parking are a short walk south.

What to Bring

Pack like you're camping

Mountain nights drop into the 40s, even in August. Plan accordingly.

Sleeping

  • Tent or RV
  • Sleeping bag rated for 40°F
  • Sleeping pad or air mattress
  • Pillow

Camp setup

  • Camp chair
  • Headlamp or flashlight
  • Cooler with ice
  • Trash bags — pack out what you pack in
  • Camp stove or grill if you want one

Food

  • Meals and snacks for your family
  • Water bottles — drinking water is available on site
  • Coffee setup

Clothing

  • Layers — warm jacket for evenings
  • Sturdy shoes for hiking
  • Swimsuit and towel for the pool or creek
  • Sunscreen and hat

Optional

  • Fishing pole and Idaho license
  • Bible
  • Instrument if you play one
  • Bug spray
No showers on site The pool and creek are it.
